Analyzing Correlations Between Health Risks



Poverty vs Healthcare

A moderately positive correlation appears to be associated with a state's percent of population in poverty and the percent of population that lacks healthcare such that the higher percent of the popultion in poverty, the higher the percent of the population that lacks healthcare.

Both Massachusetts and Texas appear to differ from the general trend. Massachusetts has a much lower percentage of the population that lacks healthcare than other states with similar percentages of the population in poverty, suggesting that Massachusetts may be more effective in providing healthcare to citizens in poverty.

Conversly, Texas has a higher percentage of population that lacks healthcare than other states with similar percentages of the population in poverty. This suggests that Texas may be less effective in providing healthcare to citizens in poverty.
